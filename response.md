## Required Features:

### Path a single piece of text takes from submission to the label an user sees

The piece of text will be accepted by an API endpoint or Content Submission Endpoint.

The submission endpoint has a rate limiting, therefore before the user submits a piece of text, the endpoint first passes through the rate limiter, checks whether the number of submissions allowed has been exceeded, or if the size of the text is within the limit, if so, a rate limit will return, and the detction will not run

When the text goes into the API, it goes through the multi-signal detection pipeline
(the pipeline has at least 2 distinct signals to classify content - yet to be determined)

which evaluates the text, and generates a confidence score with uncertainty.

Then the transparency label is created and displayed to an user on the platform, which communicates the attribution result with three label variants (high-confidence AI, high-confidence human, uncertain), as well as current status (Ex. completed). Ex. {"attribution": "likely human", "label-details": "Generated by human with high confidence", "confidence-score": 0.90, "status": "completed"}

Note: Throughout the process, every attribution decision: Ex. confidence score, signal used, and any new appeals will be captured in a structured audit log

The API endpoint returns a structured response with:

- attribution result
- confidence score
- the transparency label text

With the result displayed, the creator is able to contest the classification result, the appeal:

- captures the creator's reasoning
- log the appeal alongside the original decision
- update the content's status to "under review"

Note: Any new appeals will be captured in a structured audit log

2 Detection Signals:
LLM-based classification (Groq):
What property of the text it measures: Assess whether the text reads as human or AI generated based on the overall semantic, structural, and stylistic patterns
Why that property differs between human and AI writing: AI generated text often exhibits recurring stylistic patterns, highly consistent organizations, and predictable phrasing learned from large training datasets. While human writing may contain individual variation, imperfect flow, and context specific stylistic word choices
What it can't capture (blind spot): The classifier may misclassify highly polished human writing as AI generated, or fail to detect AI generated text that has been heavily edited by humans as it relies on overall writing patterns for evaluation

Stylometric heuristics:
What property of the text it measures: Measures statistical properties of the writing - which includes sentence length variable, typo-token ratio (vocabulary diversity), punctuation density, or average sentence complexity
Why that property differs between human and AI writing: AI generated text tends to exhibit a more structured, formal style, lacking the idiosyncrasies of human writing process, and human writing is typically more variable. AI tends to produce text with fewer typos and errors compare to humans. Sometimes AI may uses uncommon vocabulary and formal phrases that are not typical in human conversations.
What it can't capture (blindspot): The heuristics detection mode captures only the statistical writing patterns rather than semantic understanding or context of the text, making them sensitive to differences in writing style, genre, and editing. Therefore stylometric features alone can be inefficient and must be combined with other detection signals.

False Positive Problem:
what happens when your system misclassifies a human writer's work? Trace that scenario through your system — how does the confidence score reflect the uncertainty, what does the label say, and how does the creator appeal?

- If the system misclassifies a human writer's work, it should take the average of both detection signal's confiden score, and if the combined confidence score is relatively low such as 0.60, it should be classified as "uncertain", so that the creator could submit an appeal to the decision, with their reason, the appeal will be logged with the original decision, and the content status should be updated to be "under review"
